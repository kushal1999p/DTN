How Your Anonymity is Protected
  • 🧅
    Tor Network
    Access via .onion address for full IP anonymisation. Three-layer encryption through Tor relays.
  • 🚫
    No Logging
    Zero server-side logs of IP, session, browser fingerprint, or timing data. Verified by independent audit.
  • 🔐
    End-to-End Encryption
    All reports encrypted with the journalist/investigator's public key. Only they can decrypt it.
  • 🧹
    Metadata Stripping
    All uploaded files are sanitized — EXIF removed from images, revision history from documents.
  • 🏦
    Air-Gapped Processing
    Investigator's inbox machine is air-gapped. No internet. Reports transferred via encrypted USB only.

    Legal Protection
    Nepal Whistleblower Protection Act 2011 applies. Independent oversight by parliamentary committee.

# Threat STRIDE Attack Vector Risk Mitigation
T1 Biometric Database Breach Disclosure SQL injection, insider threat, or direct DB access Critical Biometrics stored as one-way SHA-3 templates. Database encrypted at rest (AES-256). Access via HSM only. Zero-trust: no direct DB access from app tier.
T2 Vote Manipulation / Stuffing Tampering Compromised voting server, insider attack, replay attacks Critical Blind signature prevents double voting. Homomorphic tallying — ballots never decrypted individually. Public audit log on blockchain. Parallel paper trail in pilot phase.
T3 Whistleblower Identity Exposure Disclosure Log analysis, traffic analysis, compromised server Critical No-log policy enforced at infrastructure level. Tor .onion routing. E2E encryption. Air-gapped recipient mailbox. Regular third-party audit of no-log compliance.
T4 Identity Impersonation / Spoofing Spoofing Credential theft, SIM swap, fake biometrics, deepfake Critical Multi-factor: biometric + TOTP + hardware key. Liveness detection against deepfakes. SIM-swap alerts. Anomaly detection on login geography and behaviour.
T5 DDoS / Service Disruption on Election Day DoS Coordinated botnet attack targeting voting endpoints Critical Cloudflare DDoS protection. Rate limiting per NID. Election systems isolated from other services. Pre-vote and post-vote grace periods. Offline voting fallback at kiosks.
T6 Insider Threat — Admin Abuse Elevation of Privilege High Principle of least privilege. All admin actions logged to immutable blockchain ledger. Dual-control for sensitive operations. Regular background checks. UEBA (user behaviour analytics).
T7 Supply Chain Attack Tampering Malicious dependency, compromised CI/CD pipeline High SBOM (software bill of materials). Signed builds. Isolated build environments. DAST/SAST in CI pipeline. Third-party code audit before production.
T8 Land Registry Fraud Tampering High All land record changes written to immutable Hyperledger blockchain. Dual-authority sign-off. Citizen SMS/push notification on any change to their records.
T9 Exam Result Tampering Tampering High

BCS Deliverable — Security Report

Security Report

Sarkar Digital — Nepal Government Digital Platform. Prepared for BCS Hackathon 2025.

1. Executive Summary

Sarkar Digital is a unified digital government platform for Nepal, designed to serve 30 million citizens across federal, provincial, and local government levels. Security was embedded as a foundational design principle from day one — not bolted on. The platform handles extraordinarily sensitive data (biometrics, votes, land records, anonymous reports) requiring cryptographic guarantees, not just best-effort security. This report outlines the asset inventory, attack surface analysis, implemented security controls, and residual risks.

2. Key Assets

  • 🪪
    Citizen Biometric Database
    30M fingerprint + iris templates. Classified: TOP SECRET. Stored as one-way cryptographic templates.
  • 🗳️
    Encrypted Ballot Store
    Election ballots encrypted with election public key. Tallied homomorphically. Individual ballots never decrypted.
  • 📣
    Whistleblower Inbox
    Air-gapped, E2E encrypted. No metadata. Only accessible by designated investigator with physical access.
  • 🏠
    Land & Property Registry
    Ownership records for all Nepali land. Changes immutably logged on Hyperledger Fabric blockchain.
  • 🔑
    National Identity Keys (PKI)
    Government CA root keys stored in HSM. Citizen private keys never leave their device.
  • 📚
    Exam & Credential Records
    Academic results hashed and published to blockchain before official announcement for tamper-evidence.

3. Security Controls Implemented

  • 🔐
    AES-256-GCM Encryption at Rest
    All databases encrypted. Keys managed by HashiCorp Vault with HSM backing. Key rotation every 90 days.
  • 🛡️
    Zero-Trust Network Architecture
    No implicit trust inside the network. Every service-to-service call authenticated via mTLS. Least privilege everywhere.
  • ✍️
    Blind Signature Voting Protocol
    Cryptographically ensures one-person-one-vote without the server knowing which ballot belongs to which citizen.
  • 🧮
    Homomorphic Vote Tallying
    Ballots are summed in encrypted form. No individual ballot is ever decrypted. Only the final tally is revealed.
  • 🔗
    Immutable Audit Blockchain
    All sensitive admin actions, land record changes, and exam results written to Hyperledger Fabric for permanent, tamper-evident audit.
  • 🧅
    Tor Hidden Service for Whistleblowing
    Whistleblower portal accessible via .onion address. No IP logs. Traffic analysis resistant.
  • 🪪
    Zero-Knowledge Proofs for Identity
    Citizens can prove eligibility (e.g., "I am over 18 and registered in Province 3") without revealing their actual identity data.
  • 📱
    Multi-Factor Authentication
    Biometric + TOTP + optional FIDO2 hardware key. Adaptive MFA based on risk score of the login context.

4. Residual Risks

🟢 LOW — Session Tokens
Short-lived JWTs reduce exposure window. Risk: stolen token in 15-min window. Mitigated but not eliminated.
🟡 MEDIUM — Physical Kiosk Tampering
Government terminals in remote areas may be physically tampered with. Requires physical security controls and regular inspection.
🟡 MEDIUM — State-Level Coercion
A determined government could compel decryption under legal orders. Mitigated by multi-jurisdiction key splitting but not eliminated.
🟡 MEDIUM — Quantum Threat (Future)
Current encryption (RSA/ECC) vulnerable to future quantum computers. Migration to post-quantum cryptography (Kyber, Dilithium) planned for Phase 4.
🟠 HIGH — Human Social Engineering
Phishing, SIM-swap, and coercion of citizens remain human-layer risks. Security awareness campaigns and hardware keys recommended for high-risk users.
🟢 LOW — Biometric Spoofing
3D-printed fingerprint or deepfake attacks partially mitigated by liveness detection. Advanced PAD (Presentation Attack Detection) required ongoing update.

5. Ethical & Legal Considerations

Data Minimisation: Only data strictly necessary for each service is collected and retained. Citizens control what they share via selective disclosure with ZKPs.

Right to Erasure: Non-biometric data can be deleted on citizen request. Biometric templates, once captured, are retained for identity integrity per Nepali law.

Surveillance Prevention: Platform explicitly designed to prevent government surveillance of citizens. No movement tracking, no behavioural profiling, no cross-service correlation without judicial order.

Whistleblower Protection: Nepal Whistleblower Protection Act 2011 embedded in system design. Parliamentary oversight committee has read-only access to system logs (no content).

Digital Divide: USSD (*9595#) fallback ensures inclusion of citizens without smartphones. Physical municipality kiosks in all 753 local governments for offline access.
